What is the target patient population for this app?

The ACC BridgeAnticoag app is meant to be used in relation to non-valvular AF patients, currently taking warfarin or any of the direct oral anticoagulants, and scheduled to undergo a procedure or surgery (cardiac or otherwise).

How is this App intended to be used?

The ACC BridgeAnticoag App helps clinicians manage the anticoagulation of non-valvular AF patients when undergoing a procedure. Clinicians should use the app to assess:

  • Whether and when anticoagulation should be interrupted prior to the procedure
  • Whether and how a bridging agent should be used
  • When anticoagulation should be restarted after a procedure

Clinicians can also email themselves the results of the app evaluation to include as part of a periprocedural management plan.

Advice from the app is derived from the 2016 ACC Expert Consensus Decision Pathway for Periprocedural Management of Anticoagulation in Patients with Non-Valvular Atrial Fibrillation. The information and recommendations in this App are meant to support clinical decision making. They are not meant to represent the only or best course of care, or replace clinical judgment. Therapeutic options should be determined after discussion between the patient and their care provider.

How was this App developed?

The App was designed and vetted through collaboration with the 2 Periprocedural Management of Anticoagulation Pathway writing group. It was further refined via user testing with physicians, nurse practitioners, and cardiac and non-cardiac specialists.
